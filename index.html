Bio

I am currently the Front End Development Team Lead at NexTraq, a Michelin Company, leading a team of other developers working on the NexTraq fleet management web platform. The team does a combination of new feature development in a modern Vue SPA codebase, and maintenance/improvements to a legacy Java-driven front end. Over time, legacy features are migrated to the new codebase, preserving user workflows and adding functionality where possible.

Previously I worked as a senior web developer at Content Thread's Atlanta office, doing a combination of Vue JS and Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) development for billion dollar companies.

In both roles a large part of my job has involved hiring, onboarding and mentoring our newer developers, and working with multiple stakeholders to make sure we understand the work and are delivering the right solutions. Aside from front-end development, I've worked to help our QA and testing strategies to become more consistent and effective, and worn various other hats as needed.

Before Content Thread I worked as Program Developer at People Making Progress, managing programs that support adults with developmental disabilities at home and in their jobs. At PMP I created internal web-based tools for our management team, and public-facing web pages as needed. When I wasn not at PMP, I worked at Appalseed Productions as an office manager and the web developer for Folkmusic.com.

In 2018 I volunteered to become a co-organizer with Code for Atlanta (a Code for America Bridage), after starting a civic hacking project with them at a the first MARTA hackathon in 2016. Through Code for Atlanta I ran "Intro to Coding" classes at DisabilityLINK's regular job skills meetings and co-ran the first Behind the Button programs with TLab Game Truck, providing basic web development classes at two Atlanta Rec Centers.

Outside of development I have a lot of interest in music and writing. My BA is in English and Music, my MA is in Ethnomusicology, and I spent some of my early twenties focused on writing and directing plays, publishing poems, and organizing various concerts for my friends and I to play at around Dublin.
